Screen Printing Project

Welcome to my project page! Here is how I designed and built my custom screen print, from building the physical frame in the woodshop to editing my design in Photoshop.

Building the Frame

Making the physical screen took quite a bit of hands-on work. Here are the steps I went through:

  • First, I measured, marked, and cut the wood using the miter saw.
  • Next, I glued and stapled the wood corners together to build a sturdy frame.
  • Finally, I cut the mesh, stretched it as tight as I could over the frame, and stapled, hammered, and taped it down.

The Digital Design Process

For the artwork, I used Photoshop to prep my design so it would work perfectly with the vinyl cutter. I even recorded some B-roll of my editing process to show the cool before-and-after transitions!

  • I imported a photograph of myself and turned it into a Smart Object.
  • I used the Magic Wand tool to clear out the background and clean up the edges.
  • To get a high-contrast look, I added a Threshold adjustment layer and played with the sliders.
  • I added a Halftone filter (using the "line" pattern) so it would be much easier to weed the vinyl material later.
  • Finally, I added some text along a curved path to finish the design.

Cutting and Pressing

Once my digital file was completely ready, I exported it and sent it to the vinyl cutting machine. I carefully weeded out the negative space of my stencil, which took some patience. To finish it off, I used the heat transfer press (iron) to melt the stencil onto my mesh and used tape around the edges to block any annoying ink leaks.
